I'm a journalist + coder who covers the intersection of privacy, politics and technology.

Starting in August, I'll be working as a magic grantee at The Brown Institute for Media Innovation at Columbia University and Stanford. Our team will be working in partnership with ProPublica to develop a video game about political campaigns and micro-targeting.

I have written stories on police impersonating activists on Facebook, the weird and sometimes toxic culture of the Canadian spy agency (and its bad cafeteria food), the legal wrangles of a wealthy Iranian fugitive and on foreign lobbying in Washington.

My work has appeared in The Globe and Mail, The Toronto Star, the Columbia Journalism Review, Vice News and The Intercept.
